# Hook Writing Best Practices

This is a shared reference used by all content-creation skills (remotion-video, text-writer, image-gen, content-engine). Before writing any hook, opening line, or first-frame text, consult this guide for the target platform.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Stop the scroll in under 5 words** — the hook's only job is to make them pause
2. **Negativity bias outperforms positivity** — "I failed at X" beats "I succeeded at X" for engagement
3. **Curiosity gaps drive clicks** — tease the answer, don't give it away
4. **Specificity beats vagueness** — numbers, timeframes, and concrete outcomes
5. **Authenticity over clickbait** — promise real value, not empty hype
6. **Contrarian views grab attention** — challenge what everyone assumes is true

## Hook Formulas (Cross-Platform)

These patterns work everywhere. Adapt tone and length per platform.

| Formula | Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Negativity + Stat** | "I failed at X — here's why N% do too." | "I failed at growing on X — here's why 95% do too." |
| **Outcome + Constraint** | "From [bad state] to [good state] without [expected method]." | "From 0 to 40k followers without showing my face." |
| **Challenge Belief** | "Everyone says [common advice], but that's [negative consequence]." | "Everyone says post daily, but that's killing your reach." |
| **Specific Result** | "The [thing] that got me [specific metric] in [timeframe]." | "The hook that got me 10k views in 24 hours." |
| **Loss Aversion** | "If you're still doing [old way], you're losing [specific cost]." | "If you're still using old funnels, you're losing $10K/month." |
| **Command + Relatability** | "Stop scrolling if you [pain point] — here's the fix." | "Stop scrolling if you hate bad skin — here's the fix." |
| **Question + Aspiration** | "What if you could [desirable outcome] tomorrow?" | "What if you could quit your job tomorrow?" |
| **Contrarian + Metric** | "[Common practice] is dead — here's what replaced it (and [result])." | "Cold calling is dead — here's what replaced it (and grew my sales 300%)." |
| **Targeted Pain** | "The #1 mistake [audience] make with [topic]." | "The #1 mistake B2B founders make with their first 100 clients." |
| **Story + Negativity** | "I almost quit [thing] — until [turning point]." | "I almost quit Facebook ads — until this hack saved my business." |

## Platform-Specific Guidelines

### X (Twitter)

**Character limit**: 280 chars (or long-form with Twitter Blue)
**Scroll speed**: Fastest of all platforms — hook must work in under 5 words
**What works**: Negativity bias, curiosity gaps, bold claims, contrarian views
**Use for**: Leading into threads for deeper engagement

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Good** | "I failed at growing on X — here's why 95% do too." (Negativity + stat) |
| **Good** | "From 0 to 40k followers without showing my face." (Outcome-focused) |
| **Good** | "Everyone says post daily, but that's killing your reach." (Challenges belief) |
| **Good** | "The hook that got me 10k views in 24 hours." (Specific result) |
| **Bad** | "Tips for better growth." (Too generic) |
| **Bad** | "Consistency is key to success." (Boring, no hook) |
| **Bad** | "Check out my new post!" (Salesy, no value) |

**Rules**:
- Ultra-concise — every word must earn its place
- Avoid overused formats — find fresh angles
- Integrate hooks into threads for deeper engagement
- Authenticity trumps clickbait

---

### TikTok

**Hook window**: First 3-5 seconds of video
**Delivery**: Combine visuals + text overlays + spoken words simultaneously
**What works**: Micro-drama (quick stories), negative hooks, authentic surprises
**What's dead**: AI-generated slop, generic intros

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Good** | Visual: Quick zoom on product fail; Spoken: "I wasted $100 on this — watch why." (Negativity + demo) |
| **Good** | "From broke to 6 figures in 90 days without ads." (Outcome + timeframe) |
| **Good** | "Stop scrolling if you hate bad skin — here's the fix." (Command + relatability) |
| **Good** | "What if you could quit your job tomorrow?" (Question + aspiration) |
| **Bad** | "Today I'm talking about skincare routines." (Process-focused, no visual) |
| **Bad** | "Here's how to improve your life." (Vague, no curiosity) |
| **Bad** | "My morning routine starts with..." (Slow, predictable) |

**Rules**:
- Visual punch in the first frame — zoom, action, or unexpected image
- Text overlay reinforces spoken hook (don't rely on audio alone)
- Promise transformation or shock
- Fast-paced, never slow intros

---

### LinkedIn

**Hook window**: First 2 lines visible before "see more" truncation
**What works**: Professional value, challenge norms, stats, pain points
**What's dead**: AI-sounding fluff, corporate announcements
**Audience**: B2B professionals — credibility and metrics matter

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Good** | "Cold calling is dead — here's what replaced it (and grew my sales 300%)." (Contrarian + metric) |
| **Good** | "Why 80% of LinkedIn posts flop before 'see more'." (Stat + curiosity) |
| **Good** | "If you're still using old funnels, you're losing $10K/month." (Challenge + loss aversion) |
| **Good** | "The mistake costing B2B founders their first 100 clients." (Pain point + specificity) |
| **Bad** | "Excited to share my thoughts on marketing." (Vague excitement) |
| **Bad** | "Today I want to talk about leadership." (No curiosity) |
| **Bad** | "Posting consistently helps." (Obvious, no proof) |

**Rules**:
- Build credibility fast — tie hooks to real metrics or experience
- Human-generated > AI-sounding (LinkedIn audience is attuned to this)
- Address industry pain points with proof
- Contrarian views perform best when backed by data

---

### Facebook

**Vibe**: Community-driven, emotional, relatable
**What works**: UGC, short videos, urgency, personal stories, FOMO
**Balance**: Negativity drives engagement but balance with positivity to avoid backlash

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Good** | "I almost quit Facebook ads — until this hack saved my business." (Story + negativity) |
| **Good** | "Stop wasting time on bad habits: Here's what changed my life." (Command + transformation) |
| **Good** | "What if one post could double your group engagement?" (Question + promise) |
| **Good** | "The #1 mistake parents make on social media." (Targeted pain point) |
| **Bad** | "Check out my new product!" (Direct sell) |
| **Bad** | "Have a great day everyone!" (No value) |
| **Bad** | "Sharing some tips today." (Vague) |

**Rules**:
- Emotional or story-driven hooks work best
- Leverage FOMO and urgency
- Relatable > polished
- Community-oriented language

---

### Instagram

**Hook window**: First line of caption (before truncation) + first frame of Reels
**What works**: Personal micro-stories, narrative-driven, negativity for carousels/Reels
**Format**: Use emojis and line breaks for scannability; hooks must tease the swipe or watch

| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Good** | "I ditched my 9-5 & you won't believe what happened next!" (Story + surprise) |
| **Good** | "Breaking my bad habit of scrolling endlessly — what I learned." (Personal + lesson) |
| **Good** | "Why this trend is a total scam (my brutal confession)." (Negativity + authenticity) |
| **Good** | "From 0 to 10k followers in 3 months — without ads." (Outcome + specificity) |
| **Bad** | "Here are my tips for growth." (Generic) |
| **Bad** | "My morning routine." (Boring start) |
| **Bad** | "Today I'm talking about..." (No curiosity) |

**Rules**:
- Personal experiences > generic advice
- Narrative-driven — start a micro-story in the hook
- Tailor to visuals — hook and first image/frame must complement each other
- Emojis are okay (moderate use) — they improve scannability

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

These kill engagement on every platform:

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Tips for better [X]" | Too generic, no curiosity |
| "Today I want to talk about..." | No hook, no reason to keep reading |
| "Here's how to improve..." | Vague, no specificity |
| "Excited to share..." | Filler, no value promise |
| "Check out my new..." | Salesy, self-serving |
| "[Topic] is important" | Obvious, no tension |
| Starting with process | Nobody cares about your process, they care about results |

## Applying Hooks by Content Type

### Video (remotion-video)
- Hook is the **first scene** (first 3-5 seconds)
- If voiceover: the script's opening line IS the hook
- If text-only: the first text animation IS the hook
- Visual must complement the hook — don't just show text on a blank screen
- For TikTok/Reels: combine text overlay + spoken hook + visual action simultaneously

### Text Posts (text-writer)
- Hook is the **first line** of the post
- On Twitter: the hook IS the tweet (or first tweet of a thread)
- On LinkedIn: must work in the 2 lines before "see more"
- On Facebook: emotional/story hooks outperform informational ones

### Images (image-gen)
- Hook is the **text overlay** or the visual concept itself
- Carousel first slide: hook text that makes them swipe
- Single image: bold statement or surprising visual
- The image should create a curiosity gap on its own

### Content Engine (orchestration)
- When generating captions for calendar items, apply the platform-specific hook formula
- When briefing creation skills in orchestrated mode, include the hook in the brief
- Ensure hook consistency across multi-platform repurposing (same core hook, adapted per platform)
